Frequently Asked Questions

What levels of care do you provide?
We provide comprehensive care ranging from minimal assistance with daily activities to more intensive support for mobility issues and age-related conditions. We conduct a thorough assessment before admission to ensure we can fully meet your loved one's needs.
Are meals tailored to specific dietary requirements?
Yes. All our meals are home-cooked and can be customized to accommodate diabetic, low-sodium, pureed, or allergy-specific diets. We focus on both nutrition and flavor to ensure residents enjoy their dining experience.
What do your rates look like?
Private-pay rates at Skagit River typically start around $X,XXX per month and vary based on room type and level of assistance needed. Rates include 24/7 supervision, assistance with personal care, home-cooked meals, medication management, housekeeping, laundry, and coordination with healthcare providers. After a brief assessment call and tour, we'll provide an exact quote for your loved one's needs.
Do you accept insurance or Medicaid?
Skagit River is a private-pay adult family home. Many families use long-term care insurance to help cover costs, and we're happy to work with your insurance provider on required documentation and billing verification. Medicare and standard health insurance typically do not pay for room and board in adult family homes, but we can walk you through what may be covered and discuss any available local funding options.
How many residents live at Skagit River at one time?
We are licensed for up to 6 residents—Washington State's maximum for an adult family home. This small size lets us offer highly personalized care and a true family atmosphere.
What conditions do you specialize in?
We specialize in dementia and memory care, mobility challenges, stroke recovery, COPD and respiratory support, diabetes management, and hospice coordination. We also support residents with general aging needs, medication management, and ADL assistance. Each care plan is tailored to the individual.
How quickly can we schedule a tour?
Usually within a few days. Call us at (360) 723-2907 or submit the contact form; we'll respond within 24 hours and work with you to find a time that fits your schedule.
What happens if my loved one's care needs increase significantly?
We reassess care plans regularly and work with families and physicians when needs change. Many increased needs can be met in-house with RN delegation and outside therapy or hospice. If at any point we feel we cannot safely meet a resident's needs, we'll have an honest conversation and help with transition planning.
How do you handle medical emergencies?
Our staff is trained in First Aid and CPR. In the event of an emergency, we immediately contact emergency services (911), followed by the resident's primary physician and designated family members.
Do you coordinate with home health or hospice agencies?
Absolutely. We work closely with visiting nurses, physical therapists, and hospice care providers to ensure residents receive comprehensive medical support without having to leave the comfort of the home.
